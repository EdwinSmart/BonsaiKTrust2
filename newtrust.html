About Us

Bonsai Kintsugi Trust is rooted in the idea that what is broken can be made whole again with care, wisdom, and community. Founded in Nairobi, our trust works with grassroots organizations, schools, and artists to build resilience, preserve culture, and create opportunities for future generations.

Our name reflects our philosophy: Bonsai represents careful cultivation and patience, while Kintsugi is the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery with gold, celebrating imperfections as part of an object's history.

Our Programs

Education Access

Scholarships, school partnerships, and mentorship for children and youth in underserved communities.

Sustainable Livelihoods

Micro-grants and training for small enterprises and urban farming initiatives to foster economic resilience.

Cultural Preservation

Supporting local artists, storytellers, and cultural events to keep traditions alive for future generations.

Our Impact

Since our founding, Bonsai Kintsugi Trust has directly supported over 3,000 individuals, funded 25 community projects, and partnered with 15 schools across Nairobi and beyond. Our impact is measured not only in numbers, but in stories of resilience and renewal.
